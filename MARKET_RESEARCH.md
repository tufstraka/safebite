# Market Validation: SafeBite - Restaurant Menu Allergen Scanner

**Research Date:** 2026-03-01
**Question:** How many tools like SafeBite exist?

## The Problem Space

**Market Size:**
- 32 million Americans with food allergies
- 5-6% of adults, 8% of children (CDC)
- $25 billion annual market (food allergy management)
- Growing awareness and demand for safety tools

## Competitor Analysis

### Direct Competitors (Upload Menu Photo → Get Allergen Analysis)

**1. No Exact Match Found**
- **SafeBite's unique approach:** Upload menu photo/PDF → instant dish-by-dish analysis
- Most tools focus on OTHER use cases (see below)

### Indirect Competitors (Different Approaches)

**1. Restaurant Database Apps**
- **Examples:** AllergyEats, Spokin, AllerMenu
- **Approach:** Crowdsourced restaurant ratings for allergy-friendliness
- **Limitation:** No menu scanning, relies on user reviews
- **Gap:** Doesn't analyze specific dishes on a menu

**2. Ingredient Scanner Apps**
- **Examples:** Yuka, Fig (food scanner)
- **Approach:** Scan product barcodes for ingredient lists
- **Limitation:** Only works for packaged foods
- **Gap:** Can't scan restaurant menus (no barcodes)

**3. Recipe/Meal Planning Apps**
- **Examples:** MyFitnessPal, Noom (some have allergen filters)
- **Approach:** User logs meals, app tracks allergens
- **Limitation:** User must manually enter dishes
- **Gap:** No OCR, no instant menu analysis

**4. Restaurant Chain Apps**
- **Examples:** McDonald's, Chipotle, etc. (built-in allergen menus)
- **Approach:** Digital menus with allergen info
- **Limitation:** Only works for that specific chain
- **Gap:** Independent restaurants don't have this

**5. Google Lens / General OCR**
- **Approach:** Can read text from photos
- **Limitation:** No allergen analysis, just text extraction
- **Gap:** User must manually check ingredients
